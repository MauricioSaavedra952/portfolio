About Me

Recently graduated from my studies in Mechanical Engineering, my interests lie in robotics and design projects. In particular, my work has surrounded haptics, human-computer interaction, and bio-inspired design. My mission is to develop innovation that will advance individual human capacity.

My background has included being the steering system lead for the Carnegie Mellon Racing club, an experience that highlighted the importance of precision in manufacturing and the organized processes involved when working with large teams of peers. I translated the technical skills from this first experience into my work in the Neuromechatronics Lab at CMU. Here, I served as a research assistant to develop electromechanical hardware that contributed to the experimental setups of my supervisor, a doctoral student. From these experiments, I processed and analyzed raw data from EMG sensors and motion capture systems to create visualizations for a manuscript that is currently pending review at Science Robotics.

The projects I have led myself have focused on bio-inspired robots, such as a bionic finger with thermo-haptic feedback, and hands for a teleoperated massage robot. The due diligence and prior art searches done for these projects equipped me with a working knowledge of human physiology and of patented designs, enabling me to create my own novel inventions. Through this foundation, I am committed to realizing more of my ideas and inventing products that will complement, if not enhance, the human form and functions.
